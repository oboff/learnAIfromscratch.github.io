Intro

This is part II in the AI Tripos an ongoing workshop to learn machine/deep learning.

Outline

Most of the following has recorded lectures on YouTube or interactive notebooks, and everything can be done in the same language Julia, which is similar in syntax to Pyret from CS019 assuming you took the programming workshop. We will also use other material as recitations like the lectures from 15-388 Practical Data Science.

  • Prereqs:
    • 18.089 Review of Mathematics: a 6 week crash course in calculus and vector calculus w/intro to linear algebra
    • 18.S191 Intro Computational Thinking: taken the same time as calculus, a course on programming simulations and other scientific computing techniques needed for later machine learning algorithms
  • Learning Theory:
    • 18.065 Matrix Methods: Strang's tools to manipulate matrices, intro to probability and stats and full explanation of how deep learning works
    • 10-701 Intro Machine Learning: Everything there is to know about current learning methods at the university intro level, and some applied ML, modern regression and data analysis. Maybe CS4780 instead of 601 as he shows how to write these algorithms.
    • A review of papers from AI researchers comparing their historical ideas to modern AI
  • Optimization:
    • 18.337J Scientific Machine Learning: Applied performance engineering of ML, how to write parallel GPU code, intro to commonly used high performance matrix algorithms in research labs.
    • 18.335J Intro to Numerical Methods: Numerical Linear Algebra book w/applied optimizations like cache-oblivious algorithms, more performance engineering
    • 10-725 Non-Covex Optimization: to understand optimizing neural networks or similar kind of optimization course

If time permits CMU's course on NLP would be fun to do since it focuses on rapid prototyping and has overlap with all the above courses meaning we don't have to take all of it.

Goals

Basically 'ML engineer' meaning be able to understand current papers in AI, turn these paper into an algorithm and optimize for practical use in a business or scientific research lab setting, a high demand skill. Also, having fun building gigantic scifi-tier systems that can dynamically expand themselves as they learn.
